### Visual-Narrative Script: The Pythagorean Theorem

**Global Style Settings:**
- **Background**: `#0F172A` (Deep Midnight Blue)
- **Primary Text**: `#F8FAFC` (Off-white)
- **Color A**: `#38BDF8` (Sky Blue)
- **Color B**: `#10B981` (Emerald Green)
- **Color C**: `#F59E0B` (Amber)
- **Markers**: `#94A3B8` (Slate Gray)
- **Alert**: `#EF4444` (Soft Red)

---

### Scene 1: Concept Roadmap
**Layout**: Title at top. Checklist on the left third. Right triangle on the right third. Equation centered at the bottom.

- **Visual-Narrative Block 1**
  - **Visual**: Write Title: `\text{The Pythagorean Theorem}`.to_edge(UP). Draw a right triangle on the right side with vertices $A, B, C$ and a small Slate Gray square at the right angle.
  - **Narration**: "The Pythagorean Theorem is the foundation of geometry, relating the sides of a right triangle."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 2**
  - **Visual**: Create "Mastery Path" checklist on the left. Item 1: "Identify the Right Angle" is highlighted in White. Items 2-4 are dimmed.
  - **Narration**: "To master it, we follow four steps: Identifying the angle, mapping sides, understanding area, and final calculation."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 3**
  - **Visual**: Render the central equation at the bottom: `a^2 + b^2 = c^2`.
  - **Narration**: "This simple relationship allows us to solve for missing lengths in any right-angled space."

---

### Scene 2: Step 1 – The Right Angle
**Layout**: Split-screen. Definition Box on the left. Vector diagram on the right.

- **Visual-Narrative Block 4**
  - **Visual**: Title changes to `\text{Step 1: The Right Angle}`. Show two vectors $\vec{u}$ (Sky Blue) and $\vec{v}$ (Emerald Green) meeting at an origin at a $45^\circ$ angle.
  - **Narration**: "First, the theorem only applies to right triangles."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 5**
  - **Visual**: Rotate vector $\vec{v}$ until it is perpendicular to $\vec{u}$ ($90^\circ$). Place a Slate Gray square marker at the intersection.
  - **Narration**: "This means two sides must be orthogonal, forming a 90-degree angle."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 6**
  - **Visual**: In the Definition Box on the left, render: `\text{Orthogonality: } \theta = 90^\circ` and `\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0`. 
  - **Narration**: "Mathematically, their dot product equals zero."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 7**
  - **Visual**: On the checklist, Step 1 gets a green checkmark. Step 2 "Map the Sides" is highlighted.
  - **Narration**: "With the right angle confirmed, we can now name the components of the triangle."

---

### Scene 3: Step 2 – Hypotenuse vs. Legs
**Layout**: Large triangle centered. 

- **Visual-Narrative Block 8**
  - **Visual**: Show the triangle. Color the bottom side (leg $a$) Sky Blue and the vertical side (leg $b$) Emerald Green. Labels `a` and `b` appear.
  - **Narration**: "Next, we map the sides. The two sides forming the L-shape are the legs."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 9**
  - **Visual**: Draw an Amber arrow starting from the Slate Gray right-angle marker, pointing across the triangle to the longest side.
  - **Narration**: "Directly across from the right angle is the Hypotenuse."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 10**
  - **Visual**: Label the diagonal side `c` in Amber. Apply a glow effect to side `c` (`Indicate` or `Circumscribe`). Render the text `c > a, b` near the side.
  - **Narration**: "This is always the longest side of the triangle."

---

### Scene 4: Step 3 – Area Logic
**Layout**: Centered triangle with geometric expansions.

- **Visual-Narrative Block 11**
  - **Visual**: From Leg $a$, grow a square of side $a$ with Sky Blue fill (opacity 0.4). From Leg $b$, grow a square of side $b$ with Emerald Green fill (opacity 0.4).
  - **Narration**: "The theorem states that if we treat each side as the edge of a square..."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 12**
  - **Visual**: Pulse the Sky Blue and Emerald Green squares simultaneously. Render `\text{Area}_a + \text{Area}_b` at the bottom.
  - **Narration**: "...the combined area of the two smaller squares..."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 13**
  - **Visual**: From the Hypotenuse $c$, grow a square of side $c$ with Amber fill (opacity 0.4). Complete the equation: `\text{Area}_a + \text{Area}_b = \text{Area}_c`.
  - **Narration**: "...perfectly equals the area of the square on the hypotenuse."

---

### Scene 5: Step 4 – Calculating Lengths
**Layout**: Checklist on the left (Step 4 active). Numerical derivation on the right.

- **Visual-Narrative Block 14**
  - **Visual**: Render the initial equation: `3^2 + 4^2 = c^2`. 
  - **Narration**: "Let's solve for $c$ using a 3-4-5 triangle."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 15**
  - **Visual**: Transform `3^2` into `9` and `4^2` into `16`. Result: `9 + 16 = c^2`.
  - **Narration**: "First, square the legs. Three squared is nine, and four squared is sixteen."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 16**
  - **Visual**: Transform `9 + 16` into `25`. Result: `25 = c^2`.
  - **Narration**: "Summing these gives us twenty-five."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 17**
  - **Visual**: Surround the equation with a square root symbol: `\sqrt{25} = \sqrt{c^2}`. Transform to `\sqrt{25} = c`.
  - **Narration**: "Finally, to find the side length $c$, we take the square root of both sides."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 18**
  - **Visual**: Transform `\sqrt{25}` into `5`. Draw an Amber box around `5 = c`.
  - **Narration**: "Resulting in a hypotenuse of five."

---

### Scene 6: Generalization (Law of Cosines)
**Layout**: Centered triangle and algebraic text.

- **Visual-Narrative Block 19**
  - **Visual**: Transform the right triangle into an obtuse triangle (angle $> 90^\circ$). Render: `c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos(\theta)`.
  - **Narration**: "What if the angle isn't 90 degrees? We use the Law of Cosines."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 20**
  - **Visual**: Highlight the term `- 2ab \cos(\theta)` in Soft Red.
  - **Narration**: "Notice this extra term here."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 21**
  - **Visual**: Transform the triangle back to $90^\circ$. Change the color of `\cos(\theta)` to Slate Gray and use a `Cross` animation to strike it out. Render `\cos(90^\circ) = 0` nearby.
  - **Narration**: "When the angle is exactly 90 degrees, the cosine becomes zero..."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 22**
  - **Visual**: Fade out the struck-out term. Only `a^2 + b^2 = c^2` remains.
  - **Narration**: "...and we return to the familiar Pythagorean Theorem."

---

### Scene 7: Final Overview
**Layout**: 2x2 Grid summary.

- **Visual-Narrative Block 23**
  - **Visual**: Display 2x2 grid. 
    1. Top-Left: Triangle with 3 colored squares.
    2. Top-Right: `a^2 + b^2 = c^2`.
    3. Bottom-Left: Amber text `c = \text{Hypotenuse}`.
    4. Bottom-Right: Text `\text{Distance / Geometry}`.
  - **Narration**: "Whether you are calculating distances in a coordinate plane or building a house..."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 24**
  - **Visual**: Grid elements fade out. The formula `a^2 + b^2 = c^2` scales up to fill the center of the screen.
  - **Narration**: "...this relationship between sides remains one of the most powerful tools in mathematics."

- **Visual-Narrative Block 25**
  - **Visual**: Surround the final formula with an Amber rectangle and pulse it once.
  - **Narration**: "Remember: $a^2$ plus $b^2$ always equals $c^2$ for a right triangle."
