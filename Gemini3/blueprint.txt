### Scene 2: The Exactness Test

**Visual-Narrative Block 5**
*   **Visual**: `Title("Step 1: The Exactness Test").to_edge(UP)`. In the top right corner, a Rule Box with three unchecked boxes: `[ ] Identify M(x,y)`, `[ ] Identify N(x,y)`, `[ ] Calculate M_y and N_x`.
*   **Narration**: "Let's start our first example by following our checklist."
*   **Action**: `Write` title and `FadeIn` the Rule Box.

**Visual-Narrative Block 6**
*   **Visual**: Center the example equation: `(3x^2 + 4xy)dx + (2x^2 + 2y)dy = 0`.
*   **Narration**: "Let's look at this equation. Our M is the term attached to dx, and N is attached to dy."
*   **Action**: `Write` the equation. Then, apply Sky Blue (`#38BDF8`) to `3x^2 + 4xy` and Mint Green (`#4ADE80`) to `2x^2 + 2y`. Put a checkmark in the first two boxes of the Rule Box.

**Visual-Narrative Block 7**
*   **Visual**: Below the equation, write the formula: `M_y = N_x`.
*   **Narration**: "The condition for exactness is that the partial of M with respect to y must equal the partial of N with respect to x."
*   **Action**: `Write` the formula and `Indicate` it with a brief pulse.

**Visual-Narrative Block 8**
*   **Visual**: Show the calculation: `\frac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(3x^2 + 4xy)`.
*   **Narration**: "Differentiating M with respect to y, we treat x as a constant."
*   **Action**: `Write` the derivative setup.

**Visual-Narrative Block 9**
*   **Visual**: The term `3x^2` within the derivative becomes semi-transparent (dimmed). The `y` in `4xy` is highlighted in Yellow (`#FDE047`).
*   **Narration**: "The 3x-squared term vanishes because it doesn't contain y."
*   **Action**: Set opacity of `3x^2` to 0.3. Use `Circumscribe` on the `y` in `4xy`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 10**
*   **Visual**: The result `4x` appears. Then show the second calculation: `\frac{\partial N}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x}(2x^2 + 2y) = 4x`.
*   **Narration**: "Differentiating N with respect to x yields the same result: 4x."
*   **Action**: `Write` the result for $M_y$, then `Write` the full line for $N_x$ in one motion.

**Visual-Narrative Block 11**
*   **Visual**: Center the equality `4x = 4x` and place a large green checkmark over it. Mark the final box in the Rule Box.
*   **Narration**: "Both result in 4x, so the equation is exact."
*   **Action**: `Transform` the two results into the equality. `FadeIn` the checkmark.

### Scene 3: Potential Function Algorithm

**Visual-Narrative Block 12**
*   **Visual**: `Title("Step 2: Finding the Potential Function").to_edge(UP)`. Checklist in top right: `[x] M_y = N_x`, `[ ] Integrate M w.r.t x`, `[ ] Solve for g(y)`.
*   **Narration**: "Because it's exact, there exists a potential function Psi. We find it by integrating M with respect to x."
*   **Action**: `FadeIn` the setup: `\Psi(x,y) = \int M \, dx + g(y)`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 13**
*   **Visual**: Substitute M: `\Psi(x,y) = \int (3x^2 + 4xy) dx + g(y)`.
*   **Narration**: "We plug our expression for M into the integral."
*   **Action**: `TransformMatchingTex` from the general setup to the specific integral.

**Visual-Narrative Block 14**
*   **Visual**: Show the result of integration: `\Psi(x,y) = x^3 + 2x^2y + g(y)`.
*   **Narration**: "Integrating term-by-term, we get x-cubed plus 2-x-squared-y."
*   **Action**: `Write` the result. Use `Indicate` on `x^3` then `2x^2y`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 15**
*   **Visual**: The term `g(y)` is highlighted in Yellow (`#FDE047`). Check the second box in the checklist.
*   **Narration**: "Don't forget the constant of integration—since we integrated with respect to x, this constant is a function of y, which we call g of y."
*   **Action**: `Circumscribe` the `g(y)` term and hold for 2 seconds.

**Visual-Narrative Block 16**
*   **Visual**: Write: `\frac{\partial \Psi}{\partial y} = N`.
*   **Narration**: "To find g of y, we differentiate our new Psi with respect to y and set it equal to N."
*   **Action**: `Write` the formula below the intermediate result.

**Visual-Narrative Block 17**
*   **Visual**: Expand the differentiation: `\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(x^3 + 2x^2y + g(y)) = 2x^2 + 2y`.
*   **Narration**: "Substituting our expression for Psi on the left and our original N on the right."
*   **Action**: `Write` the equation.

**Visual-Narrative Block 18**
*   **Visual**: Apply `Cross` to the `2x^2` on the left side and the `2x^2` on the right side.
*   **Narration**: "Terms involving x should cancel out here. If they don't, check your previous derivative!"
*   **Action**: `Create` two red crosses over the identical terms.

**Visual-Narrative Block 19**
*   **Visual**: Show the simplified equation: `g'(y) = 2y`. Then `g(y) = y^2`. Check the final box in the checklist.
*   **Narration**: "This leaves us with g-prime of y equals 2y, which integrates easily to y-squared."
*   **Action**: `TransformMatchingTex` to the simplified form, then `Write` the result for $g(y)$.

### Scene 4: Non-Exact Case (Integrating Factors)

**Visual-Narrative Block 20**
*   **Visual**: `Title("When Exactness Fails").to_edge(UP)`. New equation: `y \, dx + (2x - ye^y) \, dy = 0`.
*   **Narration**: "What if M-sub-y doesn't equal N-sub-x? Let's check this new equation."
*   **Action**: `Write` the equation.

**Visual-Narrative Block 21**
*   **Visual**: Show: `M_y = 1` and `N_x = 2`. Below them, `1 \neq 2`.
*   **Narration**: "Here, the partial of M with respect to y is 1, but the partial of N with respect to x is 2. 1 does not equal 2."
*   **Action**: `Write` the derivatives. `FadeIn` a red 'X' over the inequality.

**Visual-Narrative Block 22**
*   **Visual**: Display a formula box:
    1. `\mu(x) = e^{\int \frac{M_y - N_x}{N} dx}`
    2. `\mu(y) = e^{\int \frac{N_x - M_y}{M} dy}`
*   **Narration**: "We need an integrating factor, mu, to force exactness."
*   **Action**: `FadeIn` the formula box.

**Visual-Narrative Block 23**
*   **Visual**: Perform calculation: `\frac{N_x - M_y}{M} = \frac{2 - 1}{y} = \frac{1}{y}`.
*   **Narration**: "We test the residuals. In this case, the second formula gives us 1 over y, which depends only on y."
*   **Action**: `Write` the calculation. Highlight the result `1/y` in Yellow (`#FDE047`).

**Visual-Narrative Block 24**
*   **Visual**: Calculate $\mu$: `\mu(y) = e^{\int \frac{1}{y} dy} = e^{\ln y} = y`.
*   **Narration**: "This gives us an integrating factor of y."
*   **Action**: `Write` the steps for $\mu(y)$. `Indicate` the final result `y`.
