### Scene 2: Standard Form & Continuity Analysis

**Visual-Narrative Block 5**
- **Narration**: "Step 1: Normalize. We must have a leading coefficient of 1 for the y-prime term."
- **Visual**: Clear the screen and write the example problem: `(x^2 - 9)y' + 2xy = \frac{1}{x-5}` with the initial condition `y(4) = 2`. Create a highlight box around the `(x^2 - 9)` term.

**Visual-Narrative Block 6**
- **Narration**: "To do this, we divide every term in the equation by the coefficient of y-prime."
- **Visual**: Animate the `(x^2 - 9)` term moving as a denominator under `2xy` and `\frac{1}{x-5}`. Transform the equation into: `y' + \frac{2x}{x^2-9}y = \frac{1}{(x-5)(x^2-9)}`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 7**

**Visual-Narrative Block 8**
- **Narration**: "Step 2: Check for discontinuities. Where do these functions blow up?"

### Scene 3: Constructing the Integrating Factor (μ)

**Visual-Narrative Block 10**
- **Narration**: "Step 3: The Integrating Factor, mu of x."
- **Visual**: Clear the screen except for the normalized equation at the top. Write the formula `\mu(x) = e^{\int p(x) dx}` in Orange (`#FB923C`).

**Visual-Narrative Block 11**
- **Narration**: "We plug in our Yellow p(x) term."
- **Visual**: Transform the formula by replacing `p(x)` with the yellow term: `\mu(x) = e^{\int \frac{2x}{x^2-9} dx}`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 12**
- **Narration**: "Using a simple u-substitution, the integral becomes the natural log of x-squared minus nine."
- **Visual**: Show the side-calculation: `u = x^2-9 \implies du = 2x dx`. Then show the integral result: `\int \frac{1}{u} du = \ln|x^2-9|`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 13**
- **Narration**: "The exponential and the log cancel out, leaving us with our multiplier."
- **Visual**: Write `\mu(x) = e^{\ln|x^2-9|}`. Draw a red strike-through line across the `e` and the `\ln`. Transform the expression into its final form: `\mu(x) = x^2-9`.

### Scene 4: General Solution Integration

**Visual-Narrative Block 14**
- **Narration**: "Step 4: Multiply the standard form by mu of x."
- **Visual**: Display the normalized equation from Scene 2. Show the term `(x^2-9)` appearing and multiplying into every term on both sides.

**Visual-Narrative Block 15**
- **Narration**: "The left side is now the perfect derivative of mu times y."
- **Visual**: Compress the left-hand side terms into the derivative notation: `\frac{d}{dx} [ (x^2-9) y ]`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 16**
- **Narration**: "On the right, terms cancel out beautifully, leaving just a simple fraction."
- **Visual**: On the right side, show `(x^2-9) \cdot \frac{1}{(x-5)(x^2-9)}`. Strike through the `(x^2-9)` in the numerator and denominator. Write the simplified right side: `\frac{1}{x-5}`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 17**
- **Narration**: "Now, we integrate both sides."
- **Visual**: Prepend integral signs and `dx` to both sides: `\int \frac{d}{dx}[(x^2-9)y] dx = \int \frac{1}{x-5} dx`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 18**
- **Narration**: "Don't forget the constant of integration, C."
- **Visual**: Write the resulting algebraic equation: `(x^2-9)y = \ln|x-5| + C`. Highlight the `+ C` in Yellow (`#FDE047`).

### Scene 5: Interval of Validity & Initial Value

**Visual-Narrative Block 19**
- **Narration**: "To find C, we use our initial condition y(4) equals 2."
- **Visual**: Isolate `y` to get the general solution: `y = \frac{\ln|x-5| + C}{x^2-9}`. Below it, substitute the values: `2 = \frac{\ln|4-5|+C}{4^2-9}`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 20**
- **Narration**: "Since the natural log of 1 is zero, C equals 14."
- **Visual**: Simplify the substitution to `2 = \frac{0 + C}{7}` and then write `C = 14`. Update the general solution: `y = \frac{\ln|x-5| + 14}{x^2-9}`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 21**
- **Narration**: "Finally: Step 5. What is the Interval of Validity?"
- **Visual**: Bring back the Number Line from Scene 2 to the center of the screen, showing the gaps at `-3`, `3`, and `5`.

**Visual-Narrative Block 22**
- **Narration**: "Our initial x is 4. We look for the largest continuous interval containing 4."
- **Visual**: Place a bright dot at the position `x = 4` on the number line. 

**Visual-Narrative Block 23**
- **Narration**: "The solution is only valid between the discontinuities at x equals 3 and x equals 5."
